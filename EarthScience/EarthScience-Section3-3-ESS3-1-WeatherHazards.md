#### Part 1: What are Weather Hazards? (20 minutes)

**Define Weather Hazard**:

**Student Writes**:
```
WEATHER HAZARD

Definition: Weather events that can harm people, damage property, or disrupt communities

Examples of Weather Hazards:
- Hurricanes: Very strong storms with high winds and heavy rain
- Tornadoes: Violently rotating columns of air
- Floods: Too much water covering normally dry land
- Droughts: Long periods with very little rain
- Blizzards: Severe snowstorms with strong winds
- Heat Waves: Dangerously hot temperatures for days
- Wildfires: Often caused/worsened by drought and wind
```

**Example Research - Hurricane**:

**What is it?**
- Large rotating storm with winds 74+ mph
- Forms over warm ocean water
- Brings high winds, heavy rain, storm surge (ocean flooding)

**Where/When?**
- Atlantic Ocean, Gulf of Mexico, Eastern Pacific
- Hurricane season: June-November
- Most common: August-October

**Impacts on People**:
- High winds damage buildings, knock down trees and power lines
- Flooding from rain and storm surge
- Power outages for days or weeks
- People may need to evacuate homes

**Real Example**:
- Hurricane Katrina (2005)
- Hit Louisiana and Mississippi
- Caused catastrophic flooding
- 1,800+ deaths, massive destruction

### Session 2: Solutions Research and Evaluation (75-90 minutes)

**Objective**: Research existing solutions to weather hazards and evaluate their effectiveness.

---

#### Part 1: Types of Solutions (15 minutes)

**Discussion**: How do people reduce weather hazard impacts?

**Three Categories of Solutions**:

**Student Records**:
```
TYPES OF WEATHER HAZARD SOLUTIONS

1. PREDICTION AND WARNING
- Goal: Give people time to prepare
- Examples:
  * Weather satellites
  * Doppler radar
  * Storm tracking
  * Emergency alert systems
  * Tornado sirens

2. PROTECTION AND PREVENTION
- Goal: Reduce damage when hazard occurs
- Examples:
  * Building codes (stronger buildings)
  * Levees and dams (flood protection)
  * Firebreaks (stop wildfire spread)
  * Storm shelters
  * Seawalls (hurricane protection)

3. PREPARATION AND RESPONSE
- Goal: Help people stay safe
- Examples:
  * Emergency kits
  * Evacuation plans
  * Emergency shelters
  * First responder training
  * Community preparedness programs
```

**Adult Asks**:
- "Which type of solution do you think is most important?"
  * All three work together!
- "Can we STOP hurricanes or tornadoes?"
  * No - but we can reduce their impacts

### Session 3: Claims and Recommendations (45-60 minutes)

**Objective**: Make and defend claim about merit of a solution using evidence.

---

#### Part 1: Making a Claim (20-25 minutes)

**Activity**: Write evidence-based claim about solution effectiveness

**Claim Framework**: Claim ‚Üí Evidence ‚Üí Reasoning

**Student Writes for Each Hazard**:

```
SOLUTION CLAIM

Hazard: _______________

CLAIM:
"The solution of [specific solution] has merit for reducing impacts of [hazard] because it [brief reason]."

EVIDENCE:
1. This solution saves lives by _____
   - Example: _____
   - Data/fact: _____

2. This solution reduces property damage by _____
   - Example: _____
   - Data/fact: _____

3. This solution is effective because _____
   - Evidence: _____
   - Has been used successfully in: _____

REASONING:
"This evidence supports my claim that [solution] has merit because [connect evidence to claim]. Even though this solution has limitations like [acknowledge drawbacks], the benefits of [list benefits] make it valuable for protecting people from [hazard]. This solution works best when combined with [other solutions]."

TRADE-OFFS:
"One limitation is _____, but this is outweighed by _____ because _____."
```

**Example Claim - Hurricane Warning Systems**:

**CLAIM**:
"The solution of hurricane tracking and early warning systems has merit for reducing impacts of hurricanes because it gives people time to evacuate and prepare."

**EVIDENCE**:
1. Warning systems save lives by giving 24-48 hours notice
   - Example: Hurricane warnings allow millions to evacuate safely
   - Fact: Death rates from hurricanes have decreased dramatically since 1950s when tracking improved

2. Warnings reduce property damage by allowing preparation
   - People can board up windows, move valuables, secure outdoor items
   - Communities can position emergency responders

3. Technology is highly effective
   - NOAA Hurricane Center tracks storms with satellites and aircraft
   - Predictions are 85%+ accurate for 3-day forecasts

**REASONING**:
"This evidence supports my claim that hurricane warning systems have merit because they directly lead to lives saved and reduced damages. Even though warnings can't stop the hurricane itself, the benefits of giving people time to prepare and evacuate make this solution extremely valuable. Warning systems work best when combined with evacuation plans and strong building codes."

## Troubleshooting

**"Weather hazards are scary for my student"**:
- Focus on solutions and hope, not fear
- Emphasize: People are working to keep us safe
- Create family plan together (empowering)
- Choose less severe examples if needed
- Keep research factual, not sensational

**"We don't have major weather hazards here"**:
- Every place has something (heat waves, winter storms, heavy rain)
- Research hazards from other regions (learning perspective)
- Focus on general principles that apply anywhere
- Compare: What solutions work for multiple hazards?

**"Solutions all seem similar"**:
- Look at details: HOW does each solution work differently?
- Consider costs, limitations, effectiveness
- Some similarity is okay - shows patterns in problem-solving
- Focus on evaluation and comparison

**"Hard to evaluate without real data"**:
- Use available evidence (news reports, research findings)
- Make informed estimates based on research
- It's okay to note: "More data would be needed to evaluate fully"
- Teaches: Real engineering requires testing and data

**"Student wants to design elaborate solution"**:
- Great! But main task is evaluating EXISTING solutions
- Can add design as extension
- Keep design simple and explainable
- Apply same evaluation criteria to original design
